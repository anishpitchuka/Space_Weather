WHAT MADE THIS SUNSPOT SO MAD?
Sunspot 4446 is emerging over the sun's southeastern limb today and it looks angry. Thierry Legault photographed the seething active region from Paris, France:
☀️

Legault took this picture on May 24th using an H-alpha filter tuned to the glow of hydrogen plasma, showing the seething magnetic canopy above the sunspot. Sunspot 4446 is the formerly-giant farside sunspot first seen last week by Solar Orbiter. It has decayed since then, but its remains still look potent.
Reversed polarity: One of the sunspot groups in the region has reversed polarity — its magnetic field is oriented opposite to what Hale's Law predicts for Solar Cycle 25. Studies show about 3% of all sunspots violate Hale's Law, and reversed polarity sunspots are twice as likely to develop complex magnetic fields and explode. A farside X-flare could be in the offing. Stay tuned.
